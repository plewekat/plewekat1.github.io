About Fish Haven, Idaho
Fish Haven, originally named Rush Creek, is an unincorporated community along the shores of Bear Lake in Bear Lake County, Idaho, United States. It is 4 km (2.5 miles) north of the Utah border.

The first settlement at Fish Haven was made in 1864. A post office called Fish Haven was established in 1867, and remained in operation until 1962. The community was so named because nearby Bear Lake is a favorite fishing spot. The noise levels in Fish Haven are especially low, as the streets are very peaceful. This unincorporated community does not contain any high schools or primary schools. Regarding eating, it is very rarely practical for property owners in Fish Haven to buy their groceries on foot.
